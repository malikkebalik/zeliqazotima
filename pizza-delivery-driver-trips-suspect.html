Suspect: Tyler Morrell Pizza Guy: Pizza Delivery Driver Trips Suspect

In a surprising turn of events, a pizza delivery driver from Pennsylvania, Tyler Morrell, found himself playing the role of a hero as he helped Police apprehend a fleeing suspect who was involved in a high-speed chase.

Morrell was on one of his many deliveries when he suddenly found himself in the middle of a Police pursuit of a stolen Kia from Pennsylvania. The car was doing approximately 75mph when it crashed on Preston Street in Middletown Township.

The 17-year-old suspect and alleged car driver got out and tried to flee the scene, but that’s when Morrell stepped in. As he walked up to the customer’s House with a pizza, he saw the suspect and stuck his leg out.

It caused the suspect to trip and fall to the ground, where the Police apprehended him. The incident was captured on the customer’s security video, and the Police hailed Morrell as a hero.

Likewise, they said his quick thinking and feet allowed them to make the arrest. Police Chief Michael Vice said, “In this case, this guy ultimately saved the day.”

Despite the commotion, Morrell still managed to deliver the pizza safely, and the customer gave him a perfect 10/10 rating for the delivery. This incident shows that heroes can come from unlikely places and do extraordinary things.

Morrell’s quick thinking and bravery in the face of danger helped bring a dangerous criminal to justice, and his actions should be celebrated.

Tyler Morrell Arrest and Charges

The suspect who Morrell apprehended has been identified as 17-year-old Jhonael Gutierrez-Tejada. Police say he was facing charges of receiving stolen property, resisting arrest, and other charges. He is currently being held on $50,000 bail.

The Police also arrested a 19-year-old passenger, Jhonael Gutierrez-Tejada, who was allegedly in the stolen car with the suspect. It is not clear what charges he is facing.
